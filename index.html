What is Scrum?

    Scrum is:
  • - a management system
  • - a framework for project management
  • - an agile methodology

So what is it?

What is a framework, project management, methodology, agile?

It's still too complicated.

In simple words, Scrum is:
a way of doing things ,
that has become ...

the radical approach to creating new software and products in the tech industry.

But why is it called "Scrum"?

The term "scrum" comes from the game of rugby
(short for "scrummage" = "scrimmage").

A scrum is a method of restarting play in rugby football
that involves:
  • players packing closely together
  • with their heads down
  • and attempting to gain possession of the ball.
  • - rugby [`rʌgbi]
  • - scrum(mage) [`skrʌm(ɪʤ)] = scrimmage [`skrimiʤ]

It looks like this

It refers to the way a team works together.
  • Careful alignment,
  • unity of purpose,
  • clarity of goal come together.

It`s the perfect metaphor for what SCRUM authors wanted teams to do.

Scrum as a faster, more reliable, more effective way to create software in the tech industry was created in the early nineties by Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland.

Why did we need this "radical approach"?
It took the creation of Scrum to put the following principles into practice:
Planning Is Useful. Blindly Following Plans Is Stupid.
Inspect and Adapt.
Change or Die.
Fail Fast So You Can Fix Early.

Main idia

At its root, Scrum is based on a simple idea:

  • Whenever you start a project,
  • why not regularly check in,
  • see if what you're doing is heading in the right direction,
  • and if it's actually what people want?

And question:

  • whether there are any ways to improve how you're doing what you're doing,
  • any ways of doing it better and faster,
  • and what might be keeping you from doing that.

That's what's called an “Inspect and Adapt” cycle.
